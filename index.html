Protein Synthesis Website

Welcome to the protein synthesis model. This page explains the key components and process involved in protein synthesis.

Defenitions

Protein Synthesis

A biological process by which cells build proteins based on genetic instructions from DNA.

Transcription

The process of copying a segment of DNA into RNA.

Translation

The process where RNA is translated into a sequence of amino acids, forming proteins.

Cell

The basic unit of life where protein synthesis takes place.

Nucleus

The organelle where DNA is located and transcription occurs.

Cytoplasm

The area outside the nucleus where translation takes place.

DNA

The molecule that carries genetic information used to create proteins.

mRNA

Messenger RNA, a molecule that carries genetic instructions from the DNA to the ribosome for protein synthesis.

rRNA (Ribosome)

Ribosomal RNA, which makes up the structure of the ribosome and facilitates protein synthesis.

tRNA

Transfer RNA, which carries amino acids to the ribosome during translation.

Codon

A sequence of three nucleotides in mRNA that corresponds to a specific amino acid.

Anticodon

A sequence of three nucleotides in tRNA that pairs with the codon of mRNA.

Amino Acid

Building blocks of proteins, linked together in a chain during translation.

Polypeptide Chain

A long chain of amino acids that forms a protein once folded into a functional shape.
